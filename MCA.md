# FLIK MCA Design Gap Analysis

**Author:** VP of Product (AI Persona)  
**Target Audience:** Engineering, Product, and Design Teams  
**Date:** March 10, 2026

## Executive Summary
Merchant Cash Advance (MCA) is a pivotal fintech offering that leverages FLIK's transactional data to provide merchants with non-dilutive working capital. This analysis explores how the MCA experience should be integrated into the merchant dashboard to ensure high adoption while maintaining clarity around repayment and eligibility.

---

## 1. The Role of MCA in Merchant Growth
For ecommerce merchants, cash flow is the #1 bottleneck. MCA serves to:
1.  **Inventory Financing**: Buying stock ahead of peak seasons (e.g., Harbolnas, Ramadan).
2.  **Marketing Expansion**: Funding ad spend for successful product lines.
3.  **Operational Resilience**: Covering short-term gaps in cash flow.

---

## 2. Competitive UX Benchmarks
To build a best-in-class capital experience, FLIK should benchmark against:
*   **Shopify Capital**: Excellence in "pre-approved" offers that appear contextually in the home dashboard.
*   **Stripe Capital**: A seamless application flow that takes minutes, not days, with repayment integrated directly into sales processing.
*   **Square Capital**: Simple, clear visualization of repayment progress and remaining balance.

---

## 3. Anticipated Product Gaps (Fintech-Specific)
*   **Eligibility Transparency**: Merchants often don't know *why* they are eligible or what they need to do to increase their limit.
*   **Repayment Clarity**: Unlike traditional loans, MCA repayments are usually a percentage of daily sales. Visualization of this "Sales vs. Repayment" relationship is often missing.
*   **Contextual Friction**: Placing the "Apply" button in a siloed menu rather than showing it when a merchant is out of stock or scaling fast.
*   **Speed to Fund**: Any manual document requirement slows down the "impulse" to finance a growth opportunity.

---

## 4. Strategic Recommendations
1.  **Contextual Offers**: Trigger "Capital Offers" when a merchant's shipping volume increases by 20% MoM.
2.  **Repayment Progress Visuals**: A circular progress bar or timeline showing "Amount Paid" vs. "Remaining Balance" to reduce merchant anxiety.
3.  **One-Click Application**: Leveraging existing "Know Your Business" (KYB) and transaction data to eliminate form-filling.
4.  **Flexible Disbursement**: Allowing merchants to choose between "Disburse to Bank" or "Add to Saldo" (for immediate shipping credit).
